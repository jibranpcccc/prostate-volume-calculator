The Guiding Principles of Perfect Interlinking

    Link Up to Your Pillar: Every calculator and article page (a "cluster page") MUST link up to its main category/pillar page.
    Link Sideways to Your Peers: Cluster pages should link to other relevant cluster pages within the same silo. This shows topical relationships.
    Link Down to Your Support: Link from pillar pages and calculators to the in-depth articles that explain concepts in more detail.
    Use Strategic Anchor Text: The clickable text of a link is a powerful SEO signal. We will use a natural mix of anchor text types.
    User Experience is Paramount: A logical link helps the user find more information. If a link feels helpful to a human, it's almost always good for SEO.
    Enable Breadcrumbs: Every single page (except the homepage) must have breadcrumbs (e.g., Home > PSA Calculators > PSA Density Calculator). This is non-negotiable.

Anchor Text Strategy: The Key to Powerful Links

To avoid over-optimization penalties and create a natural profile, we will use a mix of these anchor text types for our internal links.

    Exact-Match: The anchor text is the exact primary keyword of the target page. (e.g., "PSA density calculator"). Use this sparingly, primarily from your most relevant content.
    Partial-Match: The anchor text includes the keyword plus other words. (e.g., "tool to calculate PSA density", "learn more about our PSAd tool"). This should be your most common type.
    Branded: Uses your brand name. (e.g., "ProstateVolumeCalc's assessment tool").
    Long-Tail: A longer, more descriptive phrase. (e.g., "find out what your PSA density score means").
    Generic: Non-descriptive words. (e.g., "click here," "read more," "learn more"). Use these for clear calls-to-action.
    Naked URL: The URL itself is the link. (e.g., https://prostatevolumecalc.com/psa-calculators/psa-density-calculator/). Use very rarely.
