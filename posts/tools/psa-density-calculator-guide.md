# PSA Density Calculator: Clinical Applications and Interpretation

## Overview
PSA density (PSAD) normalizes PSA levels for prostate size, enhancing cancer detection accuracy beyond PSA alone. This calculator helps clinicians stratify cancer risk and make informed biopsy decisions.

## Clinical Significance
PSAD addresses the limitation of PSA testing in men with enlarged prostates, where BPH can artificially elevate PSA levels. By dividing PSA by prostate volume, PSAD provides better discrimination between benign and malignant conditions.

## Risk Stratification Thresholds
- **<0.10 ng/mL/cc**: Low cancer risk (NPV >95%)
- **0.10-0.15 ng/mL/cc**: Intermediate risk (consider mpMRI)
- **>0.15 ng/mL/cc**: High cancer risk (biopsy recommended)

## Age-Adjusted Considerations
- **<50 years**: Lower threshold (0.08-0.10)
- **50-70 years**: Standard threshold (0.15)
- **>70 years**: Higher threshold (0.18-0.20)

## Evidence Base
Multiple studies demonstrate PSAD's superior performance compared to PSA alone, with 70% reduction in unnecessary biopsies while maintaining 95% sensitivity for clinically significant cancer.
